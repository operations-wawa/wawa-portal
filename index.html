Booking Forecast
Forward-looking view — pacing for the current month, open nights you can still fill, and confirmed revenue for the months ahead.
Confirmed Forward Revenue
—
stays from today onward
Total revenue from all bookings whose check-in date is today or later, across every property. Shown in USD at current exchange rates. This is gross revenue — TWG fees and owner expenses are not yet deducted.
Open Nights This Month
—
across the portfolio
Nights remaining in the current month with no confirmed booking, summed across all properties. Fewer open nights = less urgency. If there are many open nights and the month is more than halfway through, consider short-notice rate promotions or minimum-stay reductions.
Revenue Opportunity
—
open nights × current rates
The maximum additional revenue if every remaining open night were filled at each property's current ADR (Average Daily Rate). Think of this as the upside ceiling — real fill rates will be lower, but it quantifies what's at stake if open nights go unsold.
Properties At Risk
—
pacing below 50% occupancy
Properties where booked nights ÷ total nights in the month is below 50%. These need immediate action — rate adjustments, promotional pushes, or direct outreach to past guests. A property consistently at-risk may need a listing audit or pricing strategy review.
🛬 Check-ins — Next 7 Days
All guests arriving within the next 7 days, across every property. Use this as your daily operations checklist — confirm cleaning is scheduled, keys or access codes are ready, and welcome messages have been sent. A gap here (no check-ins) can mean a property has a lull in bookings worth investigating.
Current Month Pacing
How each property is tracking toward the 80% occupancy target for the current month — the industry benchmark for healthy short-term rental performance.
  • Progress bar — fills as nights get booked. The tick mark shows the 80% target line.
  • ADR (Average Daily Rate) — total revenue ÷ booked nights. A higher ADR with lower occupancy can still outperform a full calendar at low rates.
  • Lead time — how many days in advance bookings are typically made. Short lead times suggest last-minute demand; long lead times suggest guests plan ahead.
  • Gap nights (shown in red) — single open nights sandwiched between two bookings. These are very hard to fill and represent small but avoidable revenue loss.
  • RevPAR — Revenue Per Available Room = ADR × Occupancy%. The truest single measure of a property's revenue performance.
  • Exp ratio — operating expenses as a % of gross revenue for the month.
The calendar strip shows the full month day-by-day — booked nights are filled, open nights are hollow.
Months Ahead
Confirmed bookings for the next 3 calendar months, ranked by total nights booked. This tells you which future months already have strong demand and which need marketing attention now — before they arrive. A month with few bookings 6–8 weeks out is a warning sign; most guests book 2–6 weeks in advance for short stays.
Performance Metrics
RevPAR · platform mix · expense ratio
A cross-property benchmarking view with four key indicators:
  • Platform Mix — what share of revenue comes from each booking channel (Airbnb, Booking.com, direct, etc.). Heavy reliance on a single platform is a business risk. Aim to diversify over time.
  • RevPAR vs 80% Target — Revenue Per Available Room (ADR × occupancy%). The bar shows how close each property is to hitting the 80% occupancy benchmark. Green = at or above target; amber = 50–79%; red = below 50%.
  • Gap Nights — single-night holes between two bookings that are nearly impossible to sell. Zero gaps is ideal. If a property consistently has gaps, consider adjusting minimum-stay settings.
  • Top Channel — the platform generating the most revenue for that property this month.
  • Expense Ratio — total operating expenses (cleaning, maintenance, utilities, etc.) as a percentage of gross revenue. Below 30% is healthy; above 60% signals a cost review is needed.
Rolling 90-Day Pacing
occupancy across the last 30 days and next 60
A four-month occupancy grid showing each property's booking fill rate across the previous month, current month (◀), and the next two months. Use this to spot patterns: a property that's consistently green across all four months is a top performer; one that's red in future months needs marketing action now. The current month is highlighted. Percentages are nights booked ÷ total nights in that month.
Turnover Density
days with simultaneous check-outs + check-ins — hardest days for cleaning teams
Each box is one day of the current month. The number inside = total check-outs + check-ins across all properties on that day. Darker red = more turnovers happening simultaneously. These are your most operationally demanding days — cleaning crews need to finish one property and prepare another, often in the same afternoon window. Use this calendar to pre-assign cleaning staff, schedule extra time, and avoid under-resourcing on high-density days. Days flagged as "Busy" (3 or more turnovers) should be treated as all-hands days.
Forward Payout Schedule
projected revenue from confirmed future bookings, by property
Projected gross revenue from all confirmed future bookings (check-in date is in an upcoming month), broken down by property. This is the pre-deduction figure — TWG management fees and operating expenses are not yet subtracted. Use it to set owner expectations ahead of payout time and to identify which properties have the strongest forward booking pipeline. Properties absent from this table have no confirmed future bookings yet.
